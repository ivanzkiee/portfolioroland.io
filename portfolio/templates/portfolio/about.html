Hello, I'm Roland Ivan M. Palasigue

I'm a dedicated Computer Science student at STI College Munoz-EDSA, Quezon City, with a strong passion for web development, software engineering, and technology innovation. Currently pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, I am actively seeking On-the-Job Training opportunities where I can apply my knowledge, enhance my programming and problem-solving skills, and gain hands-on experience in a professional IT environment.

My journey in technology began with my IT studies in Mobile Application and Web Development (2020-2022), where I built a solid foundation in programming languages and web technologies. This foundation led me to develop TenantFlow, my 3rd-year Bachelor of Science in Computer Science thesis project—a comprehensive web-based rental system that demonstrates my full-stack development capabilities using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Django. Through this thesis project, I gained extensive experience in frontend design, backend logic implementation, testing, and system documentation, showcasing my ability to deliver a complete, production-ready application.

My technical expertise spans multiple programming languages including Java, Android development, C#, SQL, and web technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Django). I'm also proficient in productivity tools like Microsoft Office 365 and Google Workspace, and have experience in network troubleshooting and hardware/software maintenance.

Beyond technical skills, I've completed over 16 certifications and training programs covering diverse areas such as cybersecurity, data science, AI for business, leadership, and digital citizenship. These certifications from reputable organizations like DICT, HP Foundation, Oracle Academy, and Cisco Networking Academy reflect my commitment to continuous learning and professional development.

Leadership and collaboration are integral parts of who I am. I've been actively involved in the Central Student Government (CSG) at STI College Munoz-EDSA, progressing from Computer Science Representative (2022-2023) to Secretary (2023-2024), and currently serving as Vice President - External and Technical Committee Head (2025-Present). In these roles, I've coordinated technical requirements for campus-wide programs, managed documentation, and successfully organized events with 300+ student participants, demonstrating my ability to lead teams and execute complex projects.

My soft skills include effective leadership, time management, teamwork, and adaptability—qualities that I've developed and refined through both academic and extracurricular experiences. I believe in the power of collaboration and continuous improvement, always seeking opportunities to learn from others and contribute to meaningful projects.
